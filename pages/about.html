About Me

I am Rob Rainer, a Senior Technology Engineer with over 15 years of experience at Brookhaven National Laboratory. My expertise includes EPICS-based control systems, real-time diagnostics, and AI/ML integration.

I hold a Bachelor of Science in Physics and a Master of Science in Engineering, providing me with a strong foundation in both theoretical principles and practical applications. This academic background has been instrumental in driving my innovative contributions to accelerator operations and controls engineering.

Beyond my technical contributions, I have mentored junior engineers and interns, fostering the next generation of professionals.

My Critical Systems (EPICS-based)

As a Senior Technology Engineer, I manage several critical systems essential to the smooth operation of NSLS-II:

  • Utilities Systems: Supporting accelerator and facility performance with reliable operations.
  • Vacuum Systems: Maintaining beamline and accelerator integrity through optimized vacuum systems.
  • EPS (Equipment Protection System): Safeguarding equipment and personnel with robust protection systems.
  • PPS (Personnel Protection System): Ensuring safety across NSLS-II with secure and efficient systems.
  • Timing IOCs: Developing and maintaining systems for precise timing and synchronization of accelerator operations.
  • Operations IOCs and GUIs: Creating intuitive GUIs and tailored IOCs for streamlined operations.

My Professional Journey

My journey in science and engineering spans diverse fields, from advanced materials research to leading-edge accelerator operations.

I began as a Lab Supervisor at West Chester University of Pennsylvania, using advanced electron microscopy techniques for my undergraduate thesis on materials characterization. After earning my Bachelor of Science in Physics in 2007, I joined a Materials R&D lab, conducting research on solid oxide fuel cells (SOFCs) and photovoltaics to advance alternative energy technologies.

In 2010, I transitioned to Brookhaven National Laboratory, starting at NSLS-I as an operations coordinator and accelerator operator. During this time, I earned my Master of Science in Engineering. At NSLS-I, I supported beamline operations and coordinated experiments. With the commissioning of NSLS-II, I contributed to its *First Light* success by developing EPICS-based GUIs, commissioning the injector and storage ring, and establishing the operations center infrastructure.

Since joining the NSLS-II Accelerator Controls Group in 2020, I have taken ownership of critical accelerator systems. I have integrated modern cyber security practices, transitioned to a virtualization model, and applied AI/ML techniques to cutting-edge accelerator research.
