My Critical Systems (EPICS-based)

As a Senior Technology Engineer, I am responsible for managing several critical systems integral to the smooth operation of NSLS-II:

  • Utilities Systems: Ensuring the seamless operation of core utilities systems vital for accelerator and facility performance.
  • Vacuum Systems: Overseeing the vacuum systems which are crucial for maintaining the integrity of the accelerator and beamlines.
  • EPS (Equipment Protection System): Managing systems that safeguard both equipment and personnel in the facility.
  • PPS (Personnel Protection System): Overseeing security systems that ensure the safety of all personnel within NSLS-II.
  • Timing IOCs: Developing and maintaining systems for precise timing and synchronization of accelerator operations.
  • Operations IOCs and GUIs: Creating specialized IOCs tailored for operations use, along with Control System Studio (CSS) GUIs, to streamline operations and provide an intuitive interface for operators and system experts.

My Professional Journey

My career began as a Lab Supervisor at West Chester University of Pennsylvania, where I utilized advanced electron microscopy techniques for my undergraduate thesis on materials characterization. After earning my Bachelor of Science in Physics in 2007, I joined a Materials R&D lab, conducting research on solid oxide fuel cells (SOFCs) and photovoltaics to advance alternative energy technologies.

In 2010, I transitioned to Brookhaven National Laboratory, starting at NSLS-I as an operations coordinator and accelerator operator. I played a pivotal role in coordinating experiments and supporting beamline operations. With the commissioning of NSLS-II, I contributed to its "First Light" success by helping to develop EPICS-based GUIs, commission the injector and storage ring, and build the operations center infrastructure.

Since joining the NSLS-II Accelerator Controls Group in 2020, I have taken ownership of critical accelerator systems, integrating modern cyber security practices and transitioning to a virtualization model. My work also involves leveraging AI/ML techniques for cutting-edge accelerator research and leading system administration tasks to ensure the reliability and optimization of critical infrastructure.
